# Statistical Quality Control & Six Sigma — Bank Service Process Analysis

A structured SQC study applying Six Sigma tools to analyze and improve customer waiting time in a bank service queue system — evaluating process stability, variability, and capability against defined service standards.

---

## 🎯 Objective

Banks managing high customer footfall rarely distinguish between a process that is *stable* and one that is *capable*. A queue can operate consistently within its own variation yet still fail to meet customer expectations.

This project applies Statistical Quality Control and Six Sigma methodology to determine whether the bank's waiting time process is not just stable — but actually capable of meeting service standards.

---

## 🛠 Tools & Techniques

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Check Sheet | Structured waiting time data collection |
| Pareto Analysis | Identify major contributors to excessive wait times |
| Fishbone Diagram | Root cause analysis of service delays |
| Histogram | Distribution and variability assessment |
| X̄–R Control Charts | Process stability monitoring |
| Process Capability Analysis | Cp & Cpk measurement against specification limits |
| Power BI | Data visualization & reporting |
| Quality Frameworks | QC, QA, and ISO 9001 perspective |

---

## 📂 Data

Customer waiting time dataset structured into rational subgroups for X̄–R control chart analysis, covering multiple service windows, time periods, and queue conditions to capture real operational variability.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

**Process stability:** Statistically stable — no out-of-control points on control charts.

**Process capability:** Not meeting service specifications consistently.

| Metric | Value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Cp | 0.58 | Process spread exceeds specification width |
| Cpk | 0.40 | Process is off-center and underperforming |

A Cp and Cpk below 1.0 means the process cannot reliably serve customers within defined time limits — even when it appears "in control."

**Primary root cause:** Long queues identified as the dominant contributor to excessive waiting time.

**Critical improvement areas:**
- Variability reduction in service delivery
- Improved staffing allocation during peak periods
- Better demand-resource alignment across service windows

---

## 💡 Core Insight

> A process can be statistically stable yet still fail to meet customer expectations.  
> Capability — not just control — defines service excellence.

Stability confirms the process is consistent. Capability confirms whether that consistency is actually good enough. This project demonstrates that distinction with real service data.

---

## 💼 Business Impact

- Applies Six Sigma tools in a real-world banking service environment
- Distinguishes between process stability and process capability — a gap most operations teams overlook
- Translates Cp/Cpk outputs into concrete staffing and process improvement recommendations
- Performs structured root cause analysis from data collection through to actionable strategy
- Demonstrates end-to-end SQC methodology applicable to any service queue system
